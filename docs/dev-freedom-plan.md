## Income Streams

_Ranked by hourly rate · Built for evenings & weekends_

### 01 · Senior Freelance Development — Contracts & Projects

**Highest Earner · Core Stream** — `£95/hour avg` · 10–15 hrs/wk = £3,800–5,700/mo

Your 20 years of .NET, C#, Azure and AWS experience puts you in the £65–£120/hr contract market. Platforms like Toptal, Gun.io, and Contra cater specifically to senior engineers. You can pick up short contracts (2–8 weeks) or longer rolling engagements done entirely remotely, evenings and weekends.

**How to set up this week**
- Create a Toptal profile — their vetting is tough but means £100+/hr rates. Apply via toptal.com/developers.
- Sign up to Contra.com and Gun.io — both senior-focused with no bidding wars.
- Update your LinkedIn: "Senior .NET Engineer · Azure · AWS · Available for freelance." Add Open to Work (visible to recruiters only).
- Create a simple one-page portfolio at GitHub Pages or Vercel — list 3 projects with tech stack, problem solved, and outcome.

### 02 · Umbraco CMS Websites for Local Businesses

**Fast Wins · You Already Do This** — `£2.5k/site avg` · 1 site/month = £2,500/mo

You've already built local business websites. Systemise this into a productised service. Charge £1,500–£4,000 per site (Umbraco + hosting + basic SEO). One site per month on weekends is very realistic. Target tradies, restaurants, clinics, estate agents — high-need, low-tech sectors willing to pay properly.

**How to productise this**
- Create a templated Umbraco starter pack — reusable blocks for nav, hero, about, services, contact. Deploy time drops to 8–12 hrs per site.
- Offer three tiers: Starter (£1,500), Professional (£2,500), Premium (£4,000 with SEO + 3mo support).
- Join local Facebook business groups, post 2 case studies, offer a free 30-min site audit call. Start local word-of-mouth.
- Charge 50% upfront — this funds the Tesla pot immediately on booking.

### 03 · Azure / AWS Consulting & Architecture Reviews

**Premium Niche · High Demand** — `£500/session` · 2–3 sessions/mo = £1,000–1,500

Cloud consultancy is white-hot. Companies constantly need someone to review their Azure/AWS architecture, cut costs, or migrate workloads. At your level, charge £800–£1,500/day for consulting days or £500 for a 2-hour architecture review call. This is pure knowledge monetisation — no code delivery required.

**How to position yourself**
- Create a Calendly link for "Azure Architecture Review — £500 for 90 mins." Share on LinkedIn and relevant Slack groups.
- Write 3 LinkedIn articles: cost optimisation tips, common Azure migration mistakes, .NET on AWS patterns. Establish authority.
- Target tech startups on LinkedIn who are scaling fast and will hit cloud cost pain — DM CTOs/engineering managers directly.

### 04 · Technical Content — Articles, Courses & YouTube

**Passive Builder · Long Game** — `£350/article` · Growing to £800–2k/mo

Senior devs are paid well to write technical content. LogRocket, CSS-Tricks, Auth0, and Telerik all pay £200–£500 per article. Start a YouTube channel or Udemy course on .NET 8, Azure patterns, or Umbraco — these scale into thousands per month passively once established. Write once, earn indefinitely.

**Where to start**
- Submit a pitch to LogRocket Blog (blog.logrocket.com/write-for-us) — they pay £350+ per accepted .NET or Azure article.
- Post one 800-word tutorial to dev.to weekly. Build audience. Monetise later via sponsorships.
- Record a 3-hour Udemy course: "Umbraco CMS for .NET Developers" — your exact niche, low competition, £20–50 per sale, scales forever.

### 05 · Micro-SaaS or Developer Tool

**High Ceiling · Weekend Project** — `£2k+/mo MRR` · Once built, near-passive

Build a small B2B SaaS tool in your stack solving a niche problem — an Umbraco plugin, an Azure cost dashboard, a .NET scaffolding CLI tool. Charge £29–£99/mo via Stripe. Even 30 subscribers = £870–£2,970 MRR with zero ongoing time once built. You have every skill needed to ship this.

**Where to find ideas**
- Browse r/dotnet, r/csharp, Umbraco community forums — look for repeated pain points or "I wish there was a tool that..."
- Validate the idea first: post a landing page with a waitlist before writing a line of code. If 50 people sign up, build it.
- Use Next.js frontend + C# API + Azure hosting — your exact stack. Ship an MVP in 4 weekends.

### 📋 Tax & Legal — What You Need to Know

As a UK resident doing freelance work on the side of employment, you must register as self-employed with HMRC if earnings exceed £1,000/year. Here's what to do:

- Register for Self Assessment at gov.uk/register-for-self-assessment — takes 10 minutes online.
- Open a free FreeAgent or Wave account to track all income and expenses from day one. Makes tax return trivial.
- Save 20% of gross earnings in a separate pot — this covers your income tax and NI on profits above your Personal Allowance.
- You can claim home office costs, equipment, software subscriptions, and business mileage as allowable expenses.
- Consider forming a Ltd company once monthly earnings exceed £3,000 consistently — more tax efficient, better client credibility.
